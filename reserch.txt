Block 1: Management (C-Level, Directors)
Target audience: Managing director, head of sales and marketing.

Potential roles (role titles):
- Managing Director / Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
- Chief Operating Officer (COO) / Director of Operations
- Chief Commercial Officer (CCO) / Sales & Marketing Director
- Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
- Head of Strategy & Business Development

Phase 1: Strategy, business model, and "red lines"
Step 1 (General): How do you define your company's growth strategy in the metal distribution market, and what "red lines" (absolute taboos) exist in your business model?
Step 2 (Deepening): How do you structure the sales and marketing organization so it delivers not just "any" leads, but leads aligned with your margin strategy?
Step 3 (Drilling): You have defined "red lines." Now imagine: a giant client (e.g. Boeing) offers a contract that would double your annual revenue but requires breaking a "red line" (e.g. taking on unacceptable financial risk). As CEO, would you refuse or change the rules? Justify your decision.

Phase 2: Hard decisions under uncertainty
Step 1 (General): Describe your decision-making framework when the market is in crisis (metal price spikes, supply chain disruption).
Step 2 (Deepening): Give an example of the hardest management decision you made in the past year related to financial or operational risk.
Step 3 (Drilling): It is easy to decide when there is time for analysis. What do you do when a million-euro decision must be made in two hours and you only have 30% of the information you need? Describe your on-the-fly risk assessment approach.


Block 2: Sales (Customer-facing experts)
Target audience: Technical sales director, KAM, export sales manager.

Potential roles (role titles):
- Technical Sales Director / Head of Sales
- Key Account Manager (Aerospace / Medical / Defense)
- Senior Technical Sales Engineer
- Export Sales Manager / International Sales Manager
- Business Development Manager (New Markets)

Phase 1: Qualifying complex RFQs and early risk signals
Step 1 (General): What is your process for handling a complex RFQ (request for quotation)? Which questions are critical to ask the customer upfront?
Step 2 (Deepening): Share your quoting logic. Which early signals (red flags) tell you an order will be problematic and you should walk away?
Step 3 (Drilling): You say you reject toxic opportunities. Yet it is quarter-end, the plan is short, and a major customer sends an RFQ with obvious red flags in the spec. Do you take it to hit the plan or not? How do you balance sales targets and company risk?

Phase 2: Industry standards and technical specifications
Step 1 (General): How do you read technical specifications from medical or aerospace customers?
Step 2 (Deepening): How do you translate strict standard requirements into commercial proposal language to justify price?
Step 3 (Drilling): The customer's spec contains a hidden tolerance requirement you spotted and competitors missed. Do you point it out to the customer (possibly lowering the price) or use it as an edge to win? Describe your sales ethics.

Phase 3: Export compliance and international operations
Step 1 (General): What is specific and what are the constraints for international shipments of specialty alloys (customs, licenses)?
Step 2 (Deepening): How do you screen customers for sanctions risk (dual-use goods)?
Step 3 (Drilling): A shipment is stuck in a third country's customs due to a sudden export law change. An Asian customer stops their line and threatens penalties. What are your legal and communication steps in the first 24 hours?


Block 3: Sales Operations (Operational sales support)
Target audience: Sales operations, team assistant.

Potential roles (role titles):
- Head of Sales Operations / Sales Support Manager
- Sales Operations Specialist / Order Management Specialist
- Sales Team Assistant / Commercial Clerk
- Pricing & Quotation Specialist
- Customer Success Representative (B2B)

Phase 1: Order validation and critical data
Step 1 (General): Describe your process after sales has shaken hands with the customer. Which ERP fields are critical?
Step 2 (Deepening): What typical mistakes do salespeople make when handing orders to the back office, and what production consequences follow?
Step 3 (Drilling): You missed a sales error in the specification (e.g. wrong steel grade) and the document went to purchasing. Who bears financial responsibility in your system, and how do you resolve the conflict inside the team?


Block 4: Procurement (Strategic purchasing and materials)
Target audience: Strategic purchasing manager, materials expert.

Potential roles (role titles):
- Head of Procurement / Chief Procurement Officer (CPO)
- Strategic Purchasing Manager / Global Sourcing Manager
- Materials Expert / Metallurgical Engineer
- Category Manager (e.g., Titanium, Stainless Steel)
- Supplier Development Engineer

Phase 1: Supplier assessment and country risk
Step 1 (General): How do you actually evaluate and select steel mills (suppliers)? Which country risks do you factor in?
Step 2 (Deepening): Describe your negotiation strategy when the supplier is a monopolist for a given alloy.
Step 3 (Drilling): Your only low-cost titanium supplier in Asia is suddenly sanctioned or shuts down due to a lockdown. Contracts are burning. What is your heuristic (fast playbook) to replace the supplier without losing quality?

Phase 2: Material selection and equivalents
Step 1 (General): How do you handle requests for non-standard materials?
Step 2 (Deepening): Which heuristics (selection rules) do you use when finding a material equivalent (e.g. moving from ASTM to EN)?
Step 3 (Drilling): You found a perfect chemical match, but the customer's quality department rejects it for bureaucratic reasons. Material is already purchased. What do you do to minimize company loss?


Block 5: Quality & Compliance (Quality management and ESG)
Target audience: Head of QM, certification specialist, ESG / LkSG lead.

Potential roles (role titles):
- Head of Quality Management (QMB)
- Quality Assurance (QA) Manager / Quality Engineer
- Certification & Audit Specialist (ISO 9001, EN 9120)
- Compliance Officer / ESG & Supply Chain Act (LkSG) Manager
- Claim Manager / Warranty Specialist

Phase 1: Certification and traceability
Step 1 (General): How is your certificate verification process structured (e.g. EN 10204 3.1 or 3.2) at incoming inspection?
Step 2 (Deepening): How do you ensure 100% traceability from melt to finished part in the warehouse?
Step 3 (Drilling): Your system let through a forged EN 10204 certificate from a supplier. The part is already installed on the customer's aircraft. Walk through your crisis protocol including legal and reputational risk.

Phase 2: Audit-ready processes and ESG (Supply Chain Act / LkSG)
Step 1 (General): How do you design internal processes to be ready for surprise audits (audit-proof) from ISO or aerospace regulators?
Step 2 (Deepening): How do you implement LkSG (supply chain due diligence) requirements and assess ESG risk (environment, human rights) among partners?
Step 3 (Drilling): Your most profitable supplier violates environmental rules (ESG). Terminating the contract costs 20% of profit; keeping it risks a major regulator fine. What is your decision as head of compliance?


Block 6: Logistics & Operations (Logistics and warehousing)
Target audience: Head of logistics, logistics coordinator, warehouse manager.

Potential roles (role titles):
- Head of Logistics & Supply Chain
- Transport Logistics Coordinator / Freight Forwarding Specialist
- Warehouse & Inventory Manager
- Customs & Trade Compliance Specialist
- Consignment Stock / Vendor Managed Inventory (VMI) Manager

Phase 1: Routing and early risk signals
Step 1 (General): How do you choose transport and build routes for multimodal heavy-metal moves?
Step 2 (Deepening): Which tracking statuses or carrier communications are "early signals" of critical delay risk for you?
Step 3 (Drilling): A truck carrying titanium crashed and the goods are damaged. The customer runs just-in-time (no buffer) and will stop the line in 12 hours. As logistics lead, how do you solve this immediately?

Phase 2: Inventory and consignment (call-off)
Step 1 (General): What logic drives your warehouse inventory management?
Step 2 (Deepening): How do you run consignment stock (call-off orders) when the customer pulls material in partial lots as needed?
Step 3 (Drilling): A customer for whom you hold EUR 500k of specialty steel on consignment suddenly declares bankruptcy. Material sits on their site but legally belongs to you. How do you recover it physically and legally?


Block 7: Technical (Engineering and manufacturing)
Target audience: Technical team (drawing-driven processing).

Potential roles (role titles):
- Head of Production / Manufacturing Manager
- Technical Project Manager (Processing & Machining)
- Manufacturing Engineer / Process Engineer
- CNC / Machining Specialist (Design for Manufacturability)
- Work Preparation / Production Planner

Phase 1: Process selection and DFM (design for manufacturability)
Step 1 (General): How do you choose a machining technology (milling, laser, waterjet, etc.) when you receive a customer drawing?
Step 2 (Deepening): What are the most common design mistakes (DFM issues) you see in drawings from customer engineers?
Step 3 (Drilling): Sales already sold the job, but you see tolerances that are physically impossible on your equipment without ~50% scrap. How do you argue to the customer that the drawing must change without making sales look incompetent?
